# 01. Introduction to Energy Demand Forecasting

## The Role of Smart Grids
Smart grids represent the digitization of the electrical power system. Unlike traditional grids, they allow for two-way communication between the utility and the consumer. This infrastructure generates vast amounts of data, which is essential for maintaining a balance between energy generation and consumption.

## Importance of Forecasting
Accurate forecasting is critical for several reasons:
- **Operational Efficiency**: Reduces the cost of running "Peaker plants" (expensive backup plants).
- **Reliability**: Prevents blackouts by anticipating peak loads.
- **Renewable Integration**: Helps manage the variability of solar and wind energy.

## Challenges
- **Missing Data**: Sensors often fail or lose connection, leading to gaps in history.
- **Uncertainty**: Weather patterns and economic shifts can be volatile.
- **Noise**: Real-world data is often messy and requires sophisticated numerical handling.
